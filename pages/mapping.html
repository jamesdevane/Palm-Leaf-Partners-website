The Foundation

Why Address Mapping Comes First

The DRC is the second-largest country in Africa, home to over 100 million people — yet the vast majority of its addresses are informal, inconsistent, or entirely absent. Without a standardized address system, emergency services cannot find you, commerce cannot reach you, and government cannot serve you.

Palm Leaf Partners' National Address Mapping program is the foundational layer on which every other infrastructure project is built. It is not merely a database — it is the operating system of a modern nation.

Critically, the call center emergency dispatch system depends entirely on this address map. When a citizen dials for help, the dispatcher needs a precise, verified location. That precision is what this project delivers.

Methodology

How the Program Works

Field Survey & GPS Capture

Trained field teams equipped with GPS devices and mobile data collection tools survey every structure, road, and landmark — capturing precise coordinates for each location.

Standardized Address Assignment

Each location receives a unique, standardized address code following a national schema — province, territory, commune, street, and number — registered in the national address registry.

Post Office Hub Distribution

100 SCPT post office locations serve as the physical distribution and registration points, making the address system accessible to citizens in every province of the DRC.

Data Center Integration

All address data is stored in the Palm Leaf Partners sovereign Tier-IV Data Center, ensuring the national registry is secure, redundant, and accessible to authorized systems.

Emergency Services Linkage

The completed address database is the backbone of the regional call center dispatch system — enabling emergency responders to locate callers with precision for the first time in DRC history.

Citizen Registration

Citizens register their address through post office hubs, receiving a physical address card and digital record — creating a formal identity anchor for commerce, banking, and government services.

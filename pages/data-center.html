Sovereign Infrastructure

The DRC's Data Must Stay in the DRC

Today, the Democratic Republic of Congo's most sensitive data — government records, financial systems, telecommunications infrastructure — is largely hosted on servers outside the country. This creates a fundamental vulnerability: the DRC does not control its own digital sovereignty.

Palm Leaf Partners is building the DRC's first Tier-IV certified data center on Congolese soil. This facility will host the national address registry created by our mapping program, government cloud services, enterprise systems, and the data backbone for the call center network.

Tier-IV certification means 99.995% uptime — the highest standard in the industry, with fully fault-tolerant systems and no single point of failure.

Security Operations Center

The SOC: Always Watching, Always Protecting

Embedded within the data center is a dedicated Security Operations Center — a 24/7 command facility staffed by cybersecurity professionals monitoring every system, every connection, and every threat in real time.

The SOC protects not just the data center itself, but the entire Palm Leaf Partners infrastructure ecosystem — including the national address registry, the fiber backbone, and the call center network.

Real-Time Threat Monitoring

Continuous SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) monitoring across all connected systems with automated alerting.

Incident Response

Dedicated incident response team with documented playbooks for every threat scenario, ensuring rapid containment and recovery.

Threat Intelligence

Integration with global threat intelligence feeds to proactively identify and neutralize emerging threats before they reach DRC systems.

Compliance Reporting

Automated compliance reporting for OHADA law, DRC government requirements, and international security standards.

Hosted Systems

The Infrastructure Behind the Infrastructure

National Address Registry

The complete database of 20 million DRC addresses — the foundational dataset that powers emergency dispatch, commerce, and government services.

Government Cloud Services

Sovereign cloud hosting for DRC government ministries, agencies, and public services — keeping sensitive national data on Congolese soil.

Call Center Data Systems

The real-time dispatch database and incident management systems that power the 5–10 regional call centers, fed by the address mapping program.

Enterprise Cloud

Commercial cloud services for DRC businesses — providing local, low-latency hosting for Congolese enterprises without routing data through foreign servers.

Fiber Backbone Hub

The primary interconnection point for the national fiber backbone network, routing traffic between all major DRC cities and international connections.

Disaster Recovery

Geographically redundant disaster recovery systems ensuring that no single event can disrupt the DRC's critical national data infrastructure.

The Backbone of Digital Sovereignty

The data center is the hub that connects the address map, the fiber network, and the call centers into one coherent national system.

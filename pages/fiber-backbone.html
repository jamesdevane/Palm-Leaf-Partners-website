National Connectivity

Lighting Up the DRC

The Democratic Republic of Congo spans 2.3 million square kilometers — larger than Western Europe. Its cities are separated by vast stretches of rainforest, river, and savanna. Today, high-speed internet connectivity is available to only a small fraction of the population, concentrated in Kinshasa and a handful of other urban centers.

Palm Leaf Partners' National Fiber Backbone changes this. A high-capacity fiber optic network will connect the DRC's five major cities — Kinshasa, Lubumbashi, Goma, Kisangani, and Mbuji-Mayi — creating the arterial network on which all other digital services depend.

For the vast rural areas between these cities, Starlink satellite integration provides last-mile connectivity — ensuring that the benefits of the fiber backbone reach communities that ground-based infrastructure cannot economically serve.

Network Architecture

Primary Fiber Routes

The backbone follows the DRC's major population corridors, connecting the five largest cities and creating a resilient ring topology.

Kinshasa → Lubumbashi
Southern corridor — the DRC's economic spine connecting the capital to the mining heartland of Katanga.
Kinshasa → Kisangani
Northern corridor following the Congo River — connecting the capital to the country's geographic center.
Kisangani → Goma
Eastern corridor connecting the northern hub to the Great Lakes region and the Rwanda border.
Goma → Lubumbashi
Southeastern corridor completing the eastern ring and connecting the two major economic hubs.
Mbuji-Mayi Hub
Central node connecting to Kinshasa, Lubumbashi, and Kisangani — serving the Kasai diamond provinces.
International Gateways
Connections to submarine cable landing stations and regional fiber networks for international bandwidth.

★ Starlink Integration: Last-Mile Rural Connectivity

The DRC's rural communities — home to tens of millions of people — are separated from major cities by distances that make ground-based fiber economically unfeasible. Palm Leaf Partners is integrating Starlink low-Earth-orbit satellite connectivity to bridge this gap.

Starlink terminals deployed at SCPT post office hubs, schools, health centers, and community points provide broadband-speed internet access to communities that have never had reliable connectivity — extending the reach of the national fiber backbone to every corner of the DRC.

What the Backbone Enables

Connectivity as Infrastructure

  • Data Center Connectivity — High-capacity links to the Tier-IV data center in Kinshasa
  • Call Center Network — Low-latency connections between all regional dispatch centers
  • Address Registry Access — Real-time access to the national address database from any connected point

  • Education & Health — Telemedicine and remote education for communities across the DRC
Technical Specifications

Built for the Long Term

Single-Mode Fiber

G.652D single-mode fiber optic cable deployed in armored conduit — designed for 40+ year operational life with minimal signal degradation.

DWDM Technology

Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing enables multiple simultaneous high-capacity data streams on a single fiber pair — maximizing bandwidth efficiency.

Redundant Routing

Ring topology with automatic failover ensures that no single cable cut can disrupt the network — traffic automatically reroutes within milliseconds.
